Comparison of Low-Redshift Lyman-α Forest Observations to Hydrodynamical Simulations with Dark Photon Dark Matter

  • There is an addtional discrepancy of \(\lesssim 6.9 \mathrm{eV}\) per baryon of heating in the intergalactic medium between current observation and simulation results for Lyman-alpha absorption at \(z\approx0.1\). Such discrepancy can be explained by a dark photon heating model using hydrodynamic simulation with Gadget and Sherwood.

  • The connection between the Lyman-α forest and dark matter arises from the fact that the cosmic web is thought to be primarily made up of dark matter. The gas in the cosmic web, including the clouds responsible for the Lyman-α forest, is thought to have been drawn into the web by the gravitational attraction of the dark matter.

    In this way, studying the Lyman-α forest can provide insight into the distribution of dark matter in the universe. Observations of the Lyman-α forest have been used to constrain models of dark matter and to study the growth of structure in the universe over cosmic time.
